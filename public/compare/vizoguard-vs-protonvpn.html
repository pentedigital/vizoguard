Speed Comparison

ProtonVPN has a clear speed advantage driven by its scale. With 4,800+ servers optimized for WireGuard (its VPN Accelerator technology is specifically designed to boost throughput), ProtonVPN consistently ranks among the fastest VPNs in independent speed tests. Users in Europe and North America typically see minimal speed loss — often under 10% on a fast connection.

Vizoguard operates private, high-speed servers that are never oversold, using the Outline (Shadowsocks-based) protocol. Shadowsocks is lightweight and efficient — it was designed to minimize overhead compared to traditional VPN protocols. The Basic plan includes 100 GB/month of bandwidth. Because Vizoguard's nodes are not shared with thousands of simultaneous users, individual connection quality remains consistently high. For users who primarily want privacy and security rather than speed benchmarks across dozens of server locations, Vizoguard performs reliably. For users who need consistently fast speeds worldwide — 4K streaming, large downloads from multiple regions — ProtonVPN's infrastructure has the advantage.

Security & Encryption

Both services use strong encryption: AES-256 for symmetric encryption and forward secrecy via ephemeral key exchange. ProtonVPN's flagship protocols are WireGuard and OpenVPN, both with strong track records. Its Secure Core feature routes traffic through privacy-friendly countries (Switzerland, Iceland, Sweden) before exiting — providing double-hop protection against compromised exit nodes. This is a genuine security advantage for high-risk users.

Vizoguard's security story goes further at the endpoint level. Vizoguard Pro adds AI-powered threat detection that analyzes URLs in real time — catching brand-new phishing domains that no blocklist has catalogued. It also provides live connection monitoring (see exactly which apps are connecting to which servers) and self-healing protection that detects and restores itself if tampered with. These are endpoint security features, not just VPN add-ons. ProtonVPN's NetShield blocks known bad domains via DNS filtering, but it does not perform real-time AI analysis on new threats.

For users in censored regions: Vizoguard's Shadowsocks protocol is specifically designed to evade deep packet inspection firewalls, with a long track record in China and Iran. ProtonVPN's Stealth protocol (obfsproxy-based) also resists censorship effectively. Both are solid choices for circumvention users.

Logging & Privacy Policy

ProtonVPN's privacy credentials are genuinely strong. Founded by CERN scientists, based in Geneva under Swiss law, and subject to Swiss privacy regulations that are stricter than EU GDPR in some respects — ProtonVPN operates outside the EU and US data retention frameworks. Its no-logs policy has been independently audited by SEC Consult (2022) and Securitum, and its apps are fully open source so anyone can verify what the client does. ProtonVPN has a decade-long history of resisting government data requests, including protecting journalists and activists. This is not marketing — it is a documented track record.

Vizoguard is operated by PRIME360 HOLDING LTD in Malta (EU). It enforces a strict no-logs policy: no traffic logs, no browsing history, no connection timestamps stored. All VPN data is encrypted end-to-end and we do not have the ability to decrypt user traffic. While Vizoguard has not yet undergone a public third-party audit, it is built on Outline (developed by Jigsaw/Google) — an open-source VPN server technology.

Honest assessment: if privacy jurisdiction is your primary concern, ProtonVPN's Swiss base and decade-long track record give it a meaningful edge. If you want strong privacy alongside active AI security, Vizoguard Pro offers a different value proposition that ProtonVPN cannot match.

Streaming Support

ProtonVPN is a strong choice for streaming. Its Plus plan supports unblocking Netflix (including multiple regional libraries), Disney+, BBC iPlayer, Amazon Prime Video, Hulu, and other geo-restricted platforms. With servers optimized specifically for streaming in 100+ countries, ProtonVPN gives users access to a vast range of geographic content libraries.

Vizoguard prioritizes security over streaming geo-unblocking. With private, high-speed servers that are never oversold, it provides fast encrypted routing that will mask your location, but it focuses on security-first protection rather than streaming-specific optimization or the geographic variety needed to reliably access content from dozens of countries. If streaming is your main use case, ProtonVPN is the better tool.

Torrenting Support

Both services support torrenting (P2P traffic) over their VPN connections. ProtonVPN has dedicated P2P-optimized servers and a built-in kill switch that prevents IP exposure if the VPN drops — making it a well-regarded choice in the torrenting community. Its no-logs policy and Swiss jurisdiction also offer strong legal protection.

Vizoguard supports torrenting on all plans — all traffic, including P2P, is encrypted and routed through the VPN tunnel. The kill switch prevents IP leaks on connection drops. Vizoguard's curated server network means geographic flexibility is more limited than ProtonVPN's — you cannot select a torrent-friendly jurisdiction from a list of countries. For most users this is not an issue, but power users who need geographic flexibility for torrenting will find ProtonVPN more configurable.

Server Locations

ProtonVPN operates 4,800+ servers across 100+ countries. This scale covers every major continent and gives users access to servers in privacy-friendly jurisdictions such as Switzerland, Iceland, and Sweden — including the Secure Core locations that act as double-hop relay points. The geographic breadth is one of ProtonVPN's genuine strengths, enabling content unblocking, jurisdiction shopping, and reduced latency for users in almost any country.

Vizoguard takes a different approach: carefully selected server locations running on enterprise infrastructure (4 vCPU AMD EPYC / 16 GB RAM / 16 TB bandwidth) optimized for reliability and throughput rather than geographic spread. Because nodes are never oversold, users get consistently fast connections without the congestion common on large shared networks. Server expansion is on the roadmap. For users who do not need multi-country access — which is the majority of security-focused users — Vizoguard's private high-speed nodes deliver excellent performance. For users who need broad geographic flexibility, ProtonVPN has the clear advantage.

Apps & Platform Support

ProtonVPN covers virtually every major platform: Windows, macOS, Linux (native GUI), iOS, Android, and ChromeOS, with manual setup guides for routers and smart TVs. Its apps are fully open source and available on GitHub — every line of code is publicly auditable. ProtonVPN supports up to 10 simultaneous device connections on the Plus plan.

Vizoguard supports Mac, Windows, iOS, Android, Linux, and ChromeOS for VPN connectivity. The Vizoguard desktop app (macOS and Windows) provides the full AI security experience — threat detection, connection monitoring, phishing protection, and the VPN tunnel in one interface. Mobile users get the VPN via the standard Outline client. The Basic plan supports 1 device; the Pro plan is also designed for single-device use with the full security stack.

Both services offer a kill switch. ProtonVPN additionally supports split tunneling on all major platforms — routing some apps outside the VPN while others use it — which Vizoguard does not offer.
