Speed Comparison

ExpressVPN is consistently among the fastest VPNs in independent benchmark tests. It uses its own Lightway protocol — a lightweight, open-source protocol similar to WireGuard in design — that delivers speeds rivaling a direct connection on nearby servers. In real-world testing, ExpressVPN frequently achieves 300–600 Mbps on gigabit connections, with single-digit millisecond latency increases when connecting to nearby servers. Its global server distribution also means you are rarely far from a fast node regardless of your location.

Vizoguard uses the Shadowsocks (Outline) protocol, which adds more overhead than Lightway or WireGuard. This is a deliberate trade-off: Shadowsocks disguises VPN traffic as ordinary HTTPS, making it far harder to detect and block in restrictive networks. Speed depends significantly on your proximity to the server region. For users whose top priority is raw speed and low latency for streaming or gaming, ExpressVPN is the faster choice. For users who need a VPN that remains operational in countries like China or Iran where conventional protocols are blocked, Vizoguard's protocol approach has real advantages that speed numbers alone do not capture.

It reliably unblocks Netflix US, Netflix UK, Netflix Japan, BBC iPlayer, Disney+, Hulu, Amazon Prime Video, and YouTube Premium, among others. ExpressVPN employs dedicated streaming-optimized servers and actively works to stay ahead of VPN-detection measures used by major platforms. Users looking for the most consistent streaming experience across a wide variety of regions and platforms will find ExpressVPN hard to beat.

Vizoguard currently operates in one server region, which significantly limits geographic content unblocking. It is not designed as a streaming-first VPN — its strength is in AI-powered security, not library access across 105 countries. Users who specifically want to watch content from a different country will find ExpressVPN dramatically more capable. We think it is worth being direct here: if streaming is your primary reason for getting a VPN, ExpressVPN is the better tool. Vizoguard is the better choice if you want a VPN that also protects you from phishing, malware, and real-time threats on whichever platform you are watching.

Torrenting Support

ExpressVPN supports P2P and torrenting across all its servers — it does not restrict or route torrents to specific servers, unlike some competitors. It is based in the British Virgin Islands, a jurisdiction with no mandatory data retention laws, and its independently audited no-logs policy means no record of your activity exists. ExpressVPN does not offer port forwarding, which may slightly reduce seeding efficiency for users running torrent clients that benefit from incoming connections. For most torrenters, this is a minor limitation.

Vizoguard is not specifically optimized for torrenting. The 100 GB/month bandwidth cap on the Basic plan would be consumed quickly by active P2P users, and there is no dedicated P2P policy or optimized server infrastructure for this use case. Vizoguard's focus is on protecting everyday browsing, business communications, and sensitive transactions — not high-volume file sharing. For torrenting, ExpressVPN is the better fit between these two services. Vizoguard Pro's higher bandwidth tier is more practical for occasional P2P activity, but ExpressVPN's unlimited bandwidth and global P2P support is the stronger choice for regular torrenters.

ExpressVPN operates 3,000+ servers across 105 countries, covering every continent and major region. This network includes servers in locations that many competitors skip — including countries in Africa, Central Asia, and the Pacific. ExpressVPN uses TrustedServer technology, running all servers on RAM-only infrastructure that cannot store data permanently, and wipes all data on every reboot. This is a meaningful technical privacy guarantee on top of its geographic reach.

Vizoguard currently operates in 1 server region. This is an honest limitation we acknowledge directly. Vizoguard's infrastructure is built for security depth rather than geographic breadth — the server uses Outline (Shadowsocks), which is harder to detect and block than standard protocols. For users who need access to servers in dozens of countries for streaming, business, or geo-testing, ExpressVPN's network is substantially larger. For users who need one reliable, secure, censorship-resistant tunnel with AI security features, Vizoguard's focused approach delivers more value than a large network alone.

It supports browser extensions for Chrome, Firefox, and Edge. Crucially, ExpressVPN provides router firmware for DD-WRT, Tomato, Asus, and other routers — allowing VPN protection for smart TVs, game consoles, IoT devices, and other non-VPN-capable devices on your home network. It also supports Apple TV, Amazon Fire TV, and Android TV directly. ExpressVPN allows up to 8 simultaneous connections per account.

Vizoguard provides VPN access on Windows, macOS, iOS, Android, Linux, and ChromeOS via the Outline client. Vizoguard does not currently offer browser extensions, router-level support, or smart TV apps. For users who need VPN coverage across many device types on a single account, ExpressVPN's platform breadth and router support are genuine advantages. For users who prioritize deep security integration on their primary computer — with phishing protection, threat detection, and connection auditing — Vizoguard Pro offers features ExpressVPN cannot match at any price.
