Speed & VPN Performance

Surfshark has a clear advantage in raw VPN coverage. With over 3,200 servers across 100 countries, it offers extensive geographic flexibility, consistent streaming performance, and support for WireGuard — one of the fastest VPN protocols available. Independent tests consistently place Surfshark among the top-tier VPNs for download speeds, with minimal latency overhead even on long-distance connections. Users connecting to nearby servers typically see less than 10% speed reduction compared to an unprotected connection.

Surfshark also supports multiple protocols: WireGuard (fastest), IKEv2 (good for mobile), and OpenVPN (most compatible). Its auto-connect feature picks the fastest available server for your current location, which is particularly useful for mobile users who switch between Wi-Fi networks frequently.

Vizoguard operates private high-speed nodes using the Outline protocol (built on Shadowsocks). This protocol excels at evading deep packet inspection and is well-suited for censorship-heavy environments. Because Vizoguard's nodes are never oversold, users experience consistently low latency and reliable throughput — unlike crowded shared servers. Vizoguard Basic includes 100 GB/month bandwidth — sufficient for general browsing and security, but not ideal for heavy streaming or large file downloads. Vizoguard Pro users receive higher monthly bandwidth allocations.

The Shadowsocks protocol Vizoguard uses has a different performance profile than WireGuard. It is more computationally lightweight than OpenVPN and is optimized for low-latency communication. In regions where conventional VPN protocols are throttled or blocked, Shadowsocks can actually outperform WireGuard in real-world conditions because it completes the connection rather than being disrupted mid-session.

If your primary goal is VPN speed, server variety, or access to geo-restricted content in dozens of countries, Surfshark is the better performer. If your primary goal is threat protection with basic secure connectivity — especially in regions with censorship — Vizoguard delivers that more efficiently.

Security Features

This is the sharpest dividing line between the two services. Surfshark is fundamentally a VPN service with security add-ons. CleanWeb — included on all Surfshark plans — blocks ads, trackers, and known malware domains using static blocklists. It is a useful layer, but it relies on lists compiled after threats are discovered. Brand-new phishing domains, freshly registered for an attack campaign, will not appear on any blocklist for hours, days, or sometimes weeks after they go live. During that window, users relying on list-based protection are exposed.

Surfshark also offers an optional "Alert" feature (paid add-on) that monitors for data breaches involving your email address. This is reactive monitoring — it tells you after your data has already been compromised. It does not prevent the breach or block the attack that caused it.

Vizoguard Pro takes a fundamentally different approach with AI-powered real-time threat analysis. Every URL is evaluated before your browser loads it — including zero-day phishing domains that have never been seen before. The AI model is trained on attack patterns and behavioral signals, not just known-bad domain lists. It also includes:

  • Real-time connection monitoring — see every application on your device that is communicating with the internet, with alerts for unusual behavior. This means you can detect if an app is exfiltrating data or making unexpected connections to remote servers.
  • Self-healing protection — the security layer detects and restores itself if tampered with or disabled by malware. This is particularly important against sophisticated threats that try to disable endpoint security tools before executing their payload.
  • Malware blocking — AI-driven detection that analyzes behavioral signatures rather than relying solely on list-based filtering.

Vizoguard Basic does not include these Pro features — it provides VPN-only protection. If security depth is your primary concern, Vizoguard Pro offers endpoint security capabilities that no pure VPN service — including Surfshark — provides at any price tier. The comparison is not really VPN vs VPN; it is VPN vs VPN-plus-security-platform.

Logging & Privacy

Both Vizoguard and Surfshark enforce a zero-logging policy. Neither service records your browsing history, DNS queries, traffic content, or connection timestamps. Surfshark holds an advantage in third-party verification: it has been independently audited by Deloitte, which confirmed its no-logs infrastructure. This kind of audit matters because VPN providers can claim anything in their privacy policy — an independent audit provides evidence that the infrastructure actually matches the claims. Surfshark is incorporated in the Netherlands (EU), subject to GDPR.

Vizoguard is operated by PRIME360 HOLDING LTD, incorporated in Malta — also an EU jurisdiction with strong GDPR protections. Vizoguard enforces no-logs at the infrastructure level: VPN traffic is encrypted end-to-end using the Shadowsocks/Outline protocol, and no traffic metadata is stored on the server. Connection events are not logged. DNS queries are encrypted and not retained. As a newer service, Vizoguard has not yet undergone a formal third-party no-logs audit. For users who require audited proof of no-logging — particularly journalists, activists, or enterprise users — Surfshark currently has the verifiable track record.

That said, both services are based in GDPR jurisdictions, which provides stronger legal privacy protections than VPN providers based in the US (subject to NSLs and FISA orders), UK (Five Eyes), or other 14-Eyes countries. Neither service can be compelled to hand over logs they do not retain, and both operate under EU data protection frameworks that limit what governments can demand without judicial oversight.

One privacy consideration specific to Surfshark: it offers optional features like "Alternative ID" (a fake identity generator) and "Alert" (breach monitoring), which require providing personal data to third-party services. These features are opt-in, but users who enable them introduce additional data touchpoints outside the core VPN service.

Streaming

Surfshark is a strong performer for streaming. Its 3,200+ servers across 100 countries make it capable of unblocking Netflix libraries in multiple regions, BBC iPlayer, Hulu, Disney+, Amazon Prime Video, and most major streaming platforms. Surfshark actively works to maintain unblocking capability when streaming services update their VPN detection methods. It also supports split tunneling, which lets you route streaming traffic through the VPN while keeping other traffic on your regular connection — useful for maintaining local speeds on non-streaming activity.

Surfshark's unlimited device policy is particularly useful for streaming households: you can run Surfshark on a Smart TV, laptop, phone, and tablet simultaneously on the same subscription, without worrying about connection limits.

Vizoguard prioritizes security and speed over geo-unblocking. Its curated infrastructure prioritizes security and speed over geographic breadth. If streaming Netflix UK from the US, or accessing region-specific sports broadcasts, is a core use case for you, Surfshark is the clear choice. Vizoguard's VPN is built for private, secure browsing and threat protection — a deliberate quality-over-quantity approach that keeps nodes fast and uncrowded.

Torrenting & P2P

Surfshark supports P2P traffic on all of its servers and has dedicated optimized servers for torrenting. It includes a kill switch that cuts internet access if the VPN drops — preventing IP leaks during downloads. Combined with its no-logs policy and GDPR jurisdiction, Surfshark is a capable and well-regarded torrenting VPN. Its unlimited bandwidth means there is no cap that could interrupt an ongoing download, and unlimited device connections mean you can torrent on a desktop while using the VPN on your phone simultaneously.

Vizoguard supports general VPN traffic including P2P, but the 100 GB/month bandwidth cap on the Basic plan limits large-file use cases significantly. A single large Linux ISO or a few HD film downloads can consume most of that monthly allowance. Vizoguard Pro users receive higher bandwidth limits. Both plans include a kill switch to prevent IP exposure if the VPN connection drops unexpectedly. For heavy or regular torrenting workloads, Surfshark's unlimited bandwidth and dedicated P2P infrastructure provide a more practical setup. Vizoguard's stronger case is for users who torrent occasionally but prioritize security and phishing protection for their day-to-day browsing.
